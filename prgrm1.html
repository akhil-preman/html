HyperText Markup Language, commonly known as HTML, serves as the foundational language for structuring content on the World Wide Web. It is a markup language that utilizes a series of elements and attributes to define the various components of a webpage, such as headings, paragraphs, images, links, and tables. These elements are enclosed within tags, like p for paragraphs or h1 for a primary heading, providing semantic meaning to the content and instructing web browsers on how to display it
